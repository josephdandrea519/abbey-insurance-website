New Jersey Watercraft Insurance: Protecting Your Vessel on Our Waters

New Jersey is one of the most active boating states on the East Coast — and for good reason. With Barnegat Bay winding through Ocean County, the Raritan Bay connecting to New York Harbor, miles of pristine Jersey Shore coastline, and countless inland lakes and rivers, NJ boaters spend hundreds of thousands of hours on the water every year.

Coverage Types We Shop For You

⚖️

Liability

Covers bodily injury and property damage you cause to others on the water. Required by many marinas and strongly recommended for all boaters.

🚤

Hull & Physical Damage

Covers your vessel and its permanently attached equipment against collision, fire, theft, sinking, and storm damage. Can be written on agreed value or actual cash value basis.

🏥

Medical Payments

Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers after a boating accident, regardless of fault — critical given the physical risks on the water.

🚫

Uninsured Watercraft

Protects you if you're in an accident with an uninsured or underinsured boater. More common than you might expect on busy NJ waterways.

🛟

Towing & Assistance

Covers emergency towing to the nearest repair facility, fuel delivery, and on-water assistance when your vessel breaks down. Essential for Barnegat Bay and offshore boaters.

🎣

Personal Effects

Covers fishing gear, sporting equipment, electronics, and personal belongings aboard your vessel. Especially valuable for serious anglers with high-value tackle and equipment.

Who Needs Watercraft Insurance in NJ?

If you own any type of vessel on New Jersey waters, a dedicated watercraft policy is worth having. This includes:

  • ~Powerboats — runabouts, center consoles, and cabin cruisers used on Barnegat Bay, the Jersey Shore, and coastal NJ waters
  • ~Sailboats — from daysailers on Barnegat Bay to offshore cruising sailboats along the NJ coast
  • ~Fishing boats — from small bass boats on inland NJ lakes to offshore fishing vessels working the canyons
  • ~Jet skis & personal watercraft (PWC) — Sea-Doos, Wave Runners, and other personal watercraft are among the most common watercraft claims in NJ
  • ~Pontoon boats — popular on Barnegat Bay and inland NJ waterways for family recreation and entertaining
  • ~Kayaks and canoes with high-value equipment — serious paddlers with expensive electronics, fishing gear, or photography equipment benefit from personal effects coverage

NJ-Specific Watercraft Considerations

Boating in New Jersey comes with considerations that don't apply everywhere:

  • ~Barnegat Bay: One of the most active recreational boating areas in the Northeast — congested waterways increase collision and liability risk
  • ~Raritan Bay & Sandy Hook: Busy commercial and recreational waterways with strong currents and significant weather exposure
  • ~Marina requirements: Many marinas throughout Ocean County, Monmouth County, and the Jersey Shore require proof of liability insurance before allowing vessels to dock or store
  • ~NJ boating licensing: New Jersey requires a Boating Safety Certificate for operators born after January 1, 1978 and for all PWC operators — be sure your policy reflects your licensing status
  • ~Storm season: Northeast storm season runs from June through November — watercraft stored at exposed locations need coverage for hurricane and nor'easter damage. Sandy proved how costly coastal storms can be.
