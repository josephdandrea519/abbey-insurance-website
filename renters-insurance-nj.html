What NJ Renters Insurance Covers — And What It Doesn't

The most common misconception NJ renters have is that their landlord's insurance covers their belongings. It doesn't. Your landlord's policy covers the building's structure — walls, roof, and common areas. Your personal property, your liability, and your living expenses if you're displaced are entirely your responsibility. Renters insurance is what protects all of that — typically for less than you spend on streaming services each month.

🛋️

Personal Property

Covers your furniture, electronics, clothing, and belongings if damaged by fire, theft, windstorm, vandalism, or other covered perils. Even off-premises (e.g., theft from your car).

⚖️

Personal Liability

Covers you if someone is injured in your apartment or if you accidentally damage someone else's property. Includes legal defense costs up to your policy limit.

🏨

Loss of Use

Pays for hotel, meals, and other additional living expenses if your unit becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss like fire or major water damage.

🏥

Medical Payments

Covers minor medical bills for guests injured in your rental, regardless of fault — helps avoid small claims that could affect your rates.

💎

High-Value Item Riders

Standard policies have per-item limits. Add scheduled coverage for jewelry, art, cameras, musical instruments, and other valuables at their full appraised value.

🚫

What's NOT Covered

Flood damage, earthquake, and the building structure itself (landlord's responsibility). Separate flood coverage is available — ask us.

Why NJ Renters Skip It — And Why That's a Mistake

Most NJ renters who don't have renters insurance say it's because they assume they don't need it or can't afford it. Consider this: if a fire destroyed everything in your apartment, could you afford to replace your furniture, electronics, clothing, and kitchen appliances out of pocket? For most people, the answer is no — and that's exactly what renters insurance is for. At $10–$20/month, it's one of the best values in all of personal insurance.
