Why NJ Is Different

New Jersey Auto Insurance Is Complex — We Make It Simple

New Jersey is a no-fault insurance state, which means that after an accident, your own Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage pays your medical bills — regardless of who caused the crash. This makes NJ auto insurance structurally different from most states, and it's one reason premiums here tend to run higher than the national average.

Unlike a captive agent who can only offer one company's products, Abbey Insurance is a fully independent agency. That means whether you commute through Bergen County or live on the Jersey Shore in Ocean County, we shop your profile across competitive carriers to find the coverage that's right for your situation — and your budget.

NJ's Two Policy Types: Basic vs. Standard

New Jersey offers two distinct auto policy structures that don't exist in most other states:

  • ~
    Basic Policy: The minimum required option. Covers $15K/$30K bodily injury and $5K property damage, plus $15K PIP. Cheaper upfront but offers limited protection.
  • ~
    Standard Policy: Provides full coverage options including higher liability limits, comprehensive, collision, UM/UIM, and your choice of lawsuit threshold. Recommended for most drivers.

Lawsuit Threshold: Verbal vs. Zero

Under the Standard Policy, you choose your lawsuit threshold — a critical and often misunderstood decision. The verbal threshold (also called the limitation on lawsuit option) restricts your right to sue for pain and suffering to certain serious injuries, in exchange for a premium discount. The zero threshold preserves your full right to sue and typically costs more. Our agents will explain the real-world implications before you choose.

Coverage Types We Shop For You

🛡️

Liability

Pays for injuries and property damage you cause to others. Required in NJ. We recommend limits well above state minimums.

💥

Collision & Comprehensive

Collision covers your vehicle in an accident; comprehensive covers theft, weather, deer strikes, and other non-collision events.

🏥

PIP (No-Fault)

Required in NJ. Covers your medical bills and some lost wages after an accident regardless of fault. Multiple PIP limit options available.

🚫

UM/UIM

Uninsured & Underinsured Motorist coverage protects you when the at-fault driver has no insurance or not enough. Critical in NJ.

🔄

Gap Insurance

If your financed or leased vehicle is totaled, gap coverage pays the difference between what you owe and what your car is worth.

🚘

Rental & Roadside

Rental reimbursement and roadside assistance keep you moving when your vehicle is in the shop or stranded.
