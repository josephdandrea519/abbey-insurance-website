New Jersey Homeowners Insurance: What You Need to Know

New Jersey presents a uniquely challenging homeowners insurance landscape. Coastal exposure to Atlantic storms, older housing stock, high property values, and a history of catastrophic events like Superstorm Sandy mean that finding the right coverage — at the right price — requires more than a quick online comparison. You need an expert who knows the NJ market.

Abbey Insurance works with competitive carriers to find homeowners coverage tailored to your property, your location, and your budget. We've been doing this for New Jersey families since 1999 — and we've seen what gaps in coverage can cost when a real storm hits.

What Standard Homeowners Insurance Covers in NJ

🏗️

Dwelling

Covers the physical structure of your home — walls, roof, built-in appliances, and attached structures — against fire, wind, hail, and other covered perils.

🛋️

Personal Property

Covers your furniture, electronics, clothing, and belongings if damaged or stolen. High-value items like jewelry may need additional riders.

⚖️

Liability

Covers you if someone is injured on your property or you accidentally damage others' property. Includes legal defense costs.

🏨

Loss of Use

Pays for temporary living expenses — hotel, meals, rental — if your home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss.

🏥

Medical Payments

Covers minor medical bills for guests injured on your property, regardless of fault — helps avoid small claims.

🌊

Flood (Separate)

Standard policies do NOT cover flood. You'll need a separate NFIP or private flood policy — especially critical in coastal NJ.

Coastal NJ: Ocean, Monmouth, Atlantic & Cape May Counties

Homeowners in Ocean County, Monmouth County, Atlantic County, and Cape May County face the greatest coastal exposure in New Jersey. Wind, storm surge, and flooding are all real threats. We've seen firsthand — through Superstorm Sandy — how devastating these storms can be. Our agents help coastal homeowners:

  • ~Secure windstorm and hurricane coverage where available from standard carriers
  • ~Obtain NFIP or private flood insurance with appropriate coverage limits
  • ~Understand how wind vs. flood claims are separated (critical after Sandy)
  • ~Evaluate the NJ FAIR Plan as a last-resort market if needed

Inland NJ: Morris, Sussex, Warren, Bergen & Beyond

Homeowners in Morris, Sussex, Warren, Bergen, Essex, Hudson, Passaic, Union, Somerset, Middlesex, Mercer, and Hunterdon counties face different challenges — older homes, river flooding, high rebuilding costs, and dense suburban values. Our agents understand inland NJ's housing market and carrier preferences.

South Jersey: Burlington, Camden, Gloucester, Salem & Cumberland

South Jersey homeowners in Burlington, Camden, Gloucester, Salem, and Cumberland counties often find more competitive rates, but still need proper coverage for wind events, flooding from the Delaware River basin, and aging homes.

Condo, Townhome & Rental Property Coverage

If you own a condo or townhome, your HOA's master policy covers the building's exterior and common areas — but typically not your interior improvements or personal belongings. A condo unit owner policy (HO-6) fills those gaps. If you own a rental property, a landlord/dwelling fire policy is appropriate. Abbey handles all property types.
