Why It Matters

Why Every NJ Homeowner Needs Flood Insurance

New Jersey is consistently ranked among the top states for flood risk. From the Shore communities of Ocean and Monmouth counties, to the river corridors of Bergen, Essex, and Middlesex, to the Delaware basin towns of Burlington and Camden — flooding affects every corner of the state. And yet, standard homeowners insurance covers none of it.

Superstorm Sandy was a brutal reminder of what that gap costs. Abbey Insurance helped 200+ clients navigate Sandy claims — and the families who had flood coverage recovered. Those who didn't were left fighting with FEMA and out-of-pocket for six-figure repairs.

🚫

Your Home Policy Doesn't Cover It

Standard homeowners and renters policies explicitly exclude flood damage. If water rises from the ground up, you're not covered without a separate flood policy.

📍

It's Not Just Coastal Risk

Over 40% of flood claims nationally come from outside high-risk flood zones. River flooding, stormwater, and heavy rain affect inland NJ properties just as hard.

⏱️

There's a Waiting Period

NFIP policies have a 30-day waiting period. Don't wait for a storm to be on the radar. Private flood coverage through Neptune can have shorter waiting periods.

💰

FEMA Aid Is Not a Safety Net

Disaster assistance from FEMA averages just $5,000–$7,000 per household — rarely enough to cover serious flood damage. Insurance is the only real protection.

Know Your Options

NFIP vs. Private Flood Insurance

There are two main types of flood insurance available to NJ homeowners: the government-backed National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) and private flood insurance through carriers like Neptune. Both have pros and cons — Abbey can help you compare.

Feature NFIP (Government) Private Flood (Neptune)
Building Coverage Limit Up to $250,000 Higher limits available
Contents Coverage Limit Up to $100,000 Higher limits available
Waiting Period 30 days As low as 10 days
Loss of Use / Living Expenses ✗ Not included ✓ Often included
Basement Coverage Limited Broader options
Pricing FEMA rate tables Often more competitive
Lender-Accepted ✓ Yes ✓ Yes
